Target Unveils First Net-Zero Energy Store

Target has made a significant leap in its sustainability efforts by opening its first net-zero energy store in Vista, California.

This redesigned store incorporates over 1,800 solar panels, generating more energy than it consumes annually. This initiative is part of Target's broader goal to achieve 100% renewable energy across its operations by 2030.

Target's Ambitious Sustainability Goals

Target has set ambitious sustainability goals as part of its Target Forward strategy. By 2030, the company aims to source 100% of its electricity from renewable sources for all its operations. Target has already made significant progress, sourcing 60% of its electricity from renewable sources as of FY2022. The company is also focusing on reducing greenhouse gas emissions, with a target to achieve net-zero emissions by 2040.

Solar Power Drives Target's Renewable Energy Strategy

Target's commitment to renewable energy is evident in its extensive use of solar power. The company has installed rooftop solar panels on over 580 buildings, significantly contributing to its goal of sourcing 100% of its electricity from renewable sources by 2030. These solar installations not only reduce Target's carbon footprint but also help in achieving interim sustainability milestones.
